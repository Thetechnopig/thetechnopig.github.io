Naval Invasion of Fisherman's Row

Phase I: Tactical Analysis

Fisherman's Row Landing Zones

Based on the provided map, our primary target is Fisherman's Row. The two most viable landing zones are detailed below, along with their pros and cons. The success of the invasion depends on selecting the best approach.

Primary LZ: The Western Beach

This beach offers a wide, open landing area that can accommodate multiple landing craft simultaneously. It provides a direct path towards the town's key structures but is also exposed to potential flanking fire from fortifications on the northern peninsula.

Secondary LZ: The Southern Inlet

This landing zone is more concealed, offering a safer approach for initial disembarkment. However, the terrain is more constricted, which may make it difficult to land multiple vehicles and infantry at once, and could be a choke point if the Wardens are prepared.

Crew & Armament Analysis

  • A minimum of four crew is recommended per gunboat. More crew members are highly desirable to replace any fallen personnel during combat.
  • The Typhon gun is a superior choice for anti-personnel and consistent damage. It cannot bounce and provides more reliable DPS than the Daucus.
  • The Daucus remains the preferred weapon for destroying structures due to its high burst damage.
üéØ

Tactical Maneuvers

  • The primary goal is not to sink the enemy vessel, but to disable it. A targeted volley on the helmsman will render the boat a sitting duck.
  • Always engage enemy gunboats from the side. Their auxiliary guns have a limited arc and face the forward and rear, leaving them vulnerable to flanking maneuvers.
  • The Warden boat is faster and more agile. If they attempt to disengage and flee, do not give chase. Instead, reposition to engage them on your own terms when they are at a disadvantage.
